FEMA NOW PROMOTES "WHOLE COMMUNITY RESILIENCY"

ATTEND THIS SUMMIT

TO LEARN HOW THIS APPROACH CAN DIRECTLY BENEFIT YOUR LONG TERM CARE COMMUNITY!

 

Resiliency and restoration after an emergency are critical to protecting your residents and assuring a return to normalcy for residents and their families, for staff and for continuing the successful operation of your facility.  We recognize that communities for seniors and the frail elderly are often on their own to recover and restore their facilities. The staffs of these facilities play a critical role in the protection of the physical plant and the residents, especially during preparedness and recovery periods.

 

You are invited to a one-day seminar, which will include workshops, panel discussions, presentations on lessons learned during Sandy, and include a light lunch and networking opportunities. You will take away new ideas and new contacts, and a better understanding of how planning, communications and technology can be better used in emergencies when applied in the day to day operations of your organizations. 

 

Facility executive managers as well as maintenance and security managers are encouraged to participate in this daylong event designed as a workshop and awareness training on preparing for and recovering from the next disaster -- efficiently, effectively and for the least cost possible.

 

The day will be hosted by the New Jersey Chapter of The Alliance for Community Solutions (ACS). We are part of a growing network of long-term care communities and senior housing that are preparing to fill the gaps in emergency response. Join our group to learn how enhancing communications, using the latest preparedness tools and recognizing day to day tasks/activities can mitigate past weaknesses in emergency plans.
